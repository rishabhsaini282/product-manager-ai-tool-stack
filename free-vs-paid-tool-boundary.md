# The Free vs. Paid AI Tool Boundary for PMs

You do not always need a massive enterprise budget. Freemium and open-source AI tools deliver roughly 90% of the value of paid platforms for early-stage conceptualization[cite: 9]. However, understanding exactly where they break down is critical for maintaining velocity and InfoSec compliance[cite: 9].

## When to Use Free / Open-Source Tools
*   **Public Market Research:** Synthesizing public app store reviews or competitor pricing pages[cite: 9].
*   **Initial Feature Brainstorming:** Overcoming writer's block for user stories before entering proprietary business logic[cite: 9].
*   **Local Processing:** Deploying open-source LLMs locally to bypass token costs (requires higher technical maintenance overhead)[cite: 9].

## The Hard Limits (When to Upgrade)
1.  **Context Window Exhaustion:** Free tools lack the memory depth required to ingest hundreds of customer interview transcripts without losing context[cite: 9].
2.  **API and Integration Blocks:** Complimentary tiers cannot sync bidirectionally with Jira, Confluence, or product roadmapping software, forcing manual copy-pasting of PRDs[cite: 9].
3.  **Data Security Liabilities:** Free models train on your inputs. If you are handling proprietary roadmaps or corporate strategy, a paid enterprise tier with a zero-retention policy is a mandatory InfoSec requirement[cite: 9].
